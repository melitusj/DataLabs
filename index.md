# DataLabs: From Curiosity to Evidence

**DataLabs** is a sequence of hands-on activities that teach scientific reasoning through real and simulated data. Rather than treating *doing science* and *reading about science* as separate skills, the series presents them as a single continuous practice. Each lab moves students one step further along the same path—from noticing a phenomenon to making a defensible, evidence-based scientific claim.

The early labs follow a common throughline: **Why do parked cars get dangerously hot?** This familiar phenomenon provides a concrete context for learning abstract concepts before students apply the same reasoning and analytical skills to their own research questions and datasets.

## Learning Progression

The twelve labs build progressively across the scientific workflow:

| Topic | Focus | Labs |
|--------|-------|------|
| **Asking Questions** | Turn a raw observation into a testable scientific question. | **1A** |
| **Reading Evidence** | Interpret primary research articles and real datasets as evidence-based scientific arguments. | **1B, 2A** |
| **Finding Evidence** | Search for sources, evaluate their role in the chain from observation to claim, and locate relevant datasets. | **2B** |
| **Structuring Data** | Transform messy observations into clean, analyzable tables using **R** and the **tidyverse**. | **3A, 3B** |
| **Modeling & Inference** | Learn descriptive statistics, *t*-tests, confidence intervals, clustering, and linear regression, with emphasis on what statistical models can—and cannot—support. | **4A, 4B** |
| **Visualizing & Communicating** | Create statistical graphics and communicate evidence through Methods, Results, Discussion, and research poster formats. | **5A, 5B** |

## Accessibility

Every lab includes an accessible version built with the **maidr** package, providing screen-reader-friendly, non-visual representations of figures and statistical analyses. This allows students using assistive technologies to engage with the same concepts and evidence as their peers.

## AI-Supported Learning

A distinguishing feature of the series is that every lab contains a built-in instruction block for AI assistants. These instructions are intentionally designed to encourage **reasoning rather than answer generation**. When students use an AI assistant, it is guided to:

- Ask questions that support scientific reasoning
- Encourage interpretation rather than provide conclusions
- Help students debug their thinking without completing the work for them
- Avoid generating finished answers, analyses, or code

This approach ensures that AI functions as a learning scaffold rather than a substitute for the reasoning the labs are designed to develop.

## Intended Audience

DataLabs is designed for introductory courses in **data science**, **scientific reasoning**, or related disciplines. The materials assume **no prior experience** with statistics, programming, or research methods, making them suitable for students encountering scientific data analysis for the first time.
